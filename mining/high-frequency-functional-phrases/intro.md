# Essential Everyday Japanese Phrases (Functional & Polite Expressions)

| Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji | Literal Meaning | Function / When to Use | Notes |
|-------|----------|--------|------------------|-------------------------|-------|
| どうぞ | どうぞ | **dōzo** | “please (go ahead)” | - Offering something: コーヒーは**どうぞ**<br>- Inviting action: おはいり**ください** → どうぞ！ | Often repeated for emphasis: **Dōzo dōzo!** = “No, really, go ahead!” |
| おねがいします | おねがいします | **onegaishimasu** | “I humbly request” | - Making a request: これを かいて **ください** → おねがいします<br>- Starting an activity: しゅうり **をおねがいします**<br>- Beginning a relationship (e.g., team, class) | **Polite workhorse** — used 10+ times/day in service contexts. Shortens to **Onegai!** among friends. |
| よろしくおねがいします | よろしくおねがいします | **yoroshiku onegaishimasu** | “Please treat me favorably” | - After introductions: はじめまして、田中です。**よろしく…**<br>- Entrusting a task: しりょう、**よろしく…**<br>- Closing emails/meetings | **The #1 relationship-builder.** Casual: **Yoroshiku!** (friends); Business: full form. |
| こちらこそ | こちらこそ | **kochira koso** | “Rather, on my side…” | - Responding to thanks/apologies:<br> A: ありがとう！ → B: **Kochira koso!**<br> A: すみません → B: **Kochira koso!** | Shows humility — “*I’m* the one who should say that!” More natural than どういたしまして in many cases. |
| すみません | すみません | **sumimasen** | “It doesn’t settle (in my mind)” | - Apologizing (minor): おすし**を おとして しまいました** → すみません<br>- Getting attention: **Sumimasen**, トイレは？<br>- Expressing gratitude (when receiving help) | **More versatile than ごめんなさい** — safer for strangers. Polite default. |
| ごめんなさい | ごめんなさい | **gomen nasai** | “Please forgive” | - Apologizing (casual/friendly): to friends, family, kids | Less formal than すみません. Shortens to **Gomen!** (very casual) or **Gomennasai!** (emphatic). |
| ありがとう | ありがとう | **arigatō** | “It’s rare/difficult (so I’m grateful)” | - Thanking (casual): to friends, peers | **Arigatō gozaimasu** = polite (strangers, work). **Dōmo arigatō** = slightly more formal than plain *arigatō*. |
| どうも | どうも | **dōmo** | “In various ways” | - Light thanks: はい、**dōmo** (receiving change)<br>- Light apology: **Dōmo**, おくれて…<br>- Greeting (very casual) | The Swiss Army knife of politeness — vague but safe. Often paired: **Dōmo arigatō**, **Dōmo sumimasen**. |
| はい | はい | **hai** | “Yes” | - Acknowledging: during listening (not necessarily agreement!)<br>- Answering yes<br>- Polite attention-getter: **Hai!** (like “Yes, sir!”) | Overused by learners — in conversation, **un** (うん, casual) or nods often replace it. |
| いいえ | いいえ | **iie** | “No” | - Polite refusal: おちゃは？ → **Iie, daijōbu desu**<br>- Correcting: A: 田中さん？ → B: **Iie**, 山田です | Rarely used alone — buffer with **Daijōbu desu** (I’m fine) or **Kekkō desu** (No thanks). |
| わかりました | わかりました | **wakarimashita** | “I understood” | - Responding to instructions: 9じにきて → **Wakarimashita**<br>- Showing compliance | Shows active listening. Casual: **Wakatta!** (male), **Wakatta yo** (emphatic). |
| ちょっと | ちょっと | **chotto** | “a little” | - Softening requests: **Chotto** たすけて？<br>- Hesitation: **Chotto** むずかしいです…<br>- Excuse to leave: **Chotto** いってきます | **The ultimate softener** — makes direct speech indirect and polite. |
| ゆっくり | ゆっくり | **yukkuri** | “slowly, leisurely” | - Requesting pace: **Yukkuri** はなして ください<br>- Self-instruction: **Yukkuri** あるこう | Often paired with してください. Common in customer service: ゆっくり どうぞ. |
| ゆっくり はなして ください | — | **yukkuri hanashite kudasai** | “Please speak slowly” | - When you don’t understand | **Key N3 survival phrase**. Even native speakers use this when hearing dialects! |
| えっと… | えっと… | **etto…** | — | - Filler word (like “um…”) | Used when thinking — buys time. Paired with gestures (hand wave). |
| あのう… | あのう… | **anō…** | — | - Getting attention politely (like “Excuse me…”) | Less abrupt than すみません. Used before questions to strangers. |
| そうですか | そうですか | **sō desu ka** | “Is that so?” | - Reacting to new info (neutral) | **Sō desu ne** = “Yeah, right?” (agreement). **Sō nan desu ka** = “Oh really?!” (surprise). |
| すごい | すごい | **sugoi** | “amazing, intense” | - Reacting to news/sights: 新しい車？ → **Sugoi!** | Originally い-adj (凄い), now used as interjection. Casual: **Suge!** (male). |
| たいへんですね | — | **taihen desu ne** | “That’s tough, isn’t it?” | - Empathizing: with someone tired/busy | **Essential for building rapport**. Shows emotional intelligence. |
| おつかれさまです | おつかれさまです | **otsukaresama desu** | “You must be tired” | - Greeting coworkers (day’s end)<br>- Acknowledging effort | **Workplace staple**. Casual: **Otsukare!** (friends/peers). Never use to superiors casually. |
| いってきます | いってきます | **ittekimasu** | “I’ll go and come back” | - Leaving home/work | Standard home phrase. Response: **Itterasshai!** (Have a good trip!) |
| ただいま | ただいま | **tadaima** | “I’ve just returned” | - Returning home | Response: **Okaeri nasai!** (Welcome back!) |
| いただきます | いただきます | **itadakimasu** | “I humbly receive” | - Before eating/drinking | Said alone or in group. Shows gratitude for the meal/life. |
| ごちそうさまでした | ごちそうさまでした | **gochisōsama deshita** | “It was a feast” | - After finishing a meal | Said to host/cook. Essential etiquette. |
| おねがいできますか | おねがいできますか | **onegaidekimasu ka** | “Can I request…?” | - Polite request: ～を おねがいできますか？ | Softer than してください. Used in service: レシート **を おねがいできますか？** |
| だいじょうぶです | だいじょうぶです | **daijōbu desu** | “It’s okay” | - Reassurance: 大丈夫ですか？ → **Daijōbu desu**<br>- Declining: おちゃは？ → **Daijōbu desu** | Universal buffer phrase. Casual: **Daijōbu!** |
| けっこうです | けっこうです | **kekkō desu** | “It’s sufficient” | - Polite refusal: もっと いりますか？ → **Kekkō desu** | More formal than *daijōbu* for declining offers. |
| おさきに しつれいします | おさきに しつれいします | **osaki ni shitsurei shimasu** | “I’ll rudely leave first” | - Leaving office before others | Standard work phrase. Response: **Otsukaresama desu**. |
| よろしくおねがいいたします | — | **yoroshiku onegai itashimasu** | “I very humbly request” | - Formal/business version of *yoroshiku…* | Used in presentations, formal emails, high-stakes meetings. |
| ごめんください | ごめんください | **gomen kudasai** | “Please forgive (my intrusion)” | - Entering someone’s home/shop | Said *before* entering. Response: **Hai, dōzo!** |

> 📌 **Why These Matter for SVO→SOV Learners**:
> - These phrases **don’t follow standard grammar rules** — they’re fossilized expressions.
> - They often **stand alone** (no subject/object needed), making them perfect “chunks” to memorize.
> - Using them correctly signals **social awareness** — more impactful than perfect verb conjugation!
> - In SOV flow, they act as **discourse anchors**:  
>   ✅ **Sumimasen**, えき は どこですか？  
>   *(Excuse me, where is the station?)*  
>   → `[Polite opener] + [Place] + は + [Question]`

### 🎯 Top 5 Survival Phrases for Real-Life Use
| Japanese | Romaji | When You’ll Need It |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| **すみません、ゆっくり はなして ください。** | *Sumimasen, yukkuri hanashite kudasai.* | When you’re lost, at a shop, or on the phone |
| **ちょっと まって ください。** | *Chotto matte kudasai.* | When you need time (e.g., reading a menu, counting change) |
| **だいじょうぶです、けっこうです。** | *Daijōbu desu, kekkō desu.* | When declining an offer politely (e.g., extra food, help) |
| **よろしくおねがいします。** | *Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.* | After introducing yourself — *the* relationship starter |
| **おつかれさまです。** | *Otsukaresama desu.* | Walking past coworkers at 6 PM — instant rapport! |
